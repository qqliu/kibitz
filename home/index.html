Instructions for Creating a Table

  1. Create a repository by entering a name in the field, "Name of the new repository:".

  2. Click on your newly created repository.

  3. Click "Files".

  4. Click on the button next to Files.

  5. Upload a comma deliminated list of items with the column names as the header. Click on the "Create Table" icon to create a table from the uploaded file.

What are recommenders?

Recommender systems provide item recommendations to you based on your preferences. If you use services like Amazon, Netflix, Google, eHarmony, Facebook, Pandora or Goodreads, you are using recommender systems! Recommenders help you find appliances, movies, articles, music, books, friends, and even potential partners. Most importantly, they help you find things that you like.



How do recommenders work?

You might really like the Harry Potter series but really dislike the Lord of the Rings trilogy. One way you can input these preferences into recommenders is by rating the Harry Potter series an 8 out of 10 but the Lord of the Rings trilogy a 4 out of 10. The recommender then matches your preferences with other users who have similar preferences. Then, it recommends to you items that these other users like. For example, other users who like Harry Potter but dislike Lord of the Rings love The Golden Compass. The Golden Compass would be presented to you as a recommendation. Recommenders do not even need your ratings to provide recommendations. They can provide recommendations based on things like what you've clicked on or how long you stayed on a page. Many researchers focus on building collaborative filtering recommender systems based on these ideas!



What is Kibitz ?

Kibitz helps you build recommenders! With Kibitz, you can:

  • Create a website that provides user recommendations without writing a single line of code.

  • Populate your recommender from a list of items.

  • Customize your recommender website using our online interface.

  • Integrate recommender functionalities into your existing website.

  • Generate item lists using our custom online scrapper.

  • Observe user like and dislike trends using data from your recommender!

Detailed Instructions

Kibitz offers two different ways to build your recommenders. To start, sign-up by following these steps:

  1. Upload your table of items on Datahub. Click the Sign-Up link and allow Kibitz access to your table.


  2. Fill in information about your table: we will display a title column, description column, and an image column by default.


  3. If you have a column that contains a numerical ranking of your items, you may include it. We will provide recommendations of items with the highest rankings (i.e. an item with a ranking of 10 will be recommended before an item of ranking 2). Duplicate rankings are allowed!


  4. Submit your information, and we create a default recommender for you! To view more customization options, log in to Kibitz using your Datahub account.


Creation Options

Ready-Built

Upload a table to Datahub or use a pre-existing table. Link your table with Kibitz, and Kibitz will give you the option of either downloading a ZIP file containing your recommender to host at your own domain or hosting your recommender at kibitz.csail.mit.edu/YOUR_USERNAME/YOUR_REPO_NAME.

Customize

Log in to customize your recommender using our online interface. You may also choose to integrate Kibitz seamlessly into your website using our easy to learn, Kibitz API.

Recommender Types

Mix, match, or use all three! The default recommender will recommend items based on user ratings and overall ranks.

User Ratings

Users create profiles and rate items. We will recommend items based on these user ratings.

Similar Items

We will provide recommendations of items that are similar to items that users like. You must provide us with a customized user similarity file. See our API for more details. (Coming soon!)

Overall Ranks

Given a column of numerical ranks of the items, the recommender will return the items with the highest rankings.

Credits

Kibitz began as a research project in Haystack, a lab in MIT CSAIL. The purpose of the project is three-fold:

  1. Study the end-to-end application building process of non/inexperienced coders.
  2. Look into whether recommendations may be made for seemingly unrelated items.
  3. Provide an end-to-end backend recommendation builder and engine with easy-to-use API that may be plugged into a variety of frontends.
